Nyege Nyege Festival is an annual electronic music festival held in Jinja, Uganda. The festival is known for its eclectic mix of genres, including traditional African music, electronic music, and experimental music. Nyege Nyege has been praised for its commitment to showcasing African talent, and it has been described as "one of the most important music festivals in Africa."

The festival was founded in 2015 by Derek Debru, a Belgian DJ and producer who has lived in Uganda for many years. Debru was inspired to start the festival after he was impressed by the Ugandan music scene. "I was blown away by the energy and creativity of the music I heard in Uganda," Debru said. "I wanted to create a festival that would celebrate this music and bring it to a wider audience."

Nyege Nyege has grown rapidly since its inception. The first festival attracted a few hundred people, but the 2022 edition attracted over 10,000 attendees. The festival has also gained international attention, and it has been featured in publications such as The Guardian, The New York Times, and Pitchfork. The festival is known for its unique atmosphere. The main stage is located on the banks of the Nile River, and the festival grounds are decorated with colorful lights and artwork. festival also features a number of non-musical attractions, such as art installations, workshops, and film screenings.

Nyege Nyege is a celebration of African music and culture. The festival is a must-attend for anyone interested in experiencing the best of African music.
